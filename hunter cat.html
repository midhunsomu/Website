A device designed to detect if a credit card reader is stealing your data.

What is it?

The Hunter Cat is a small, credit card-sized device powered by a coin battery. You swipe it into a card reader at a vape shop or gas station ATM and check the lights. A “warning” or “dangerous” light means you should use a different machine. To reset, click the button. It has a sleep function that turns it off after 15 seconds to save battery.

Made by Electronic Cats and Salvador Mendoza, the Hunter Cat uses an ATMEL SAMD11 Arm Microcontroller. It detects the number of magnetic heads in a card reader. A normal reader has one head; if it detects more, it shows a warning light, indicating a possible card skimmer.


what can it do?

The Hunter Cat can detect malicious card skimmers in ATMs, saving you from the hassle of calling your bank. However, it currently can't protect NFC card data, though the Hunter Cat NFC is available for that purpose. NFC skimming is a risk, but tap cards usually have better security than magnetic stripes, making other methods easier for thieves.

In addition to skimmers, there are devices called card "shimmers" that target the EMV chip by inserting themselves between the chip and the reader. Despite these threats, magnetic stripe skimming remains the most common and easiest method for stealing credit card info, not just at gas station pumps.


How much of a threat is it?

Many of the tools we've discussed are designed for hacking, but the Hunter Cat is meant to protect you from getting hacked. For the average consumer, it could be a lifesaver. For a scammer trying to skim your card via the magnetic stripe, it means they won't get your card info to make fraudulent purchases. Tough luck for them! They'll have to resort to other methods to steal your credit card information.

Could I use it myself?

Absolutely—it couldn't be easier. If you travel a lot or worry about getting skimmed, you'll find the Hunter Cat useful. It's affordable and non-intrusive, easily stored in your car's glove compartment or bag, though it's larger than a credit card and won't fit in your wallet. The lights are simple to use and read, making it ideal for non-hackers. Just remember, it only protects against simple magnetic stripe skimmers, and many places are phasing out magnetic stripes in favor of EMV, tap cards, and other contactless payment systems.
